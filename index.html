1960's

"In the sixties, fashion was about liberation. It was about setting women free."

The 1960's were full of experimentation of culture and Twiggy was at the forefront of fashion.

1970's

“In the Seventies, we still had dreams and hopes of Utopia.”

The 70s was a time of self expression, where women dove into finding out who they wanted to be and where fashion inspiration came from around the world.

1980's

“In a lot of places, of course, the 80s have never really come to an end.”

The 80s brought rockstar and pirate inspired fashion to the forefront, pushing with bright colors and eccentric textures.

1990's

“The '90s were extremely diverse, almost like a laboratory of the new century.”

Experimentation in fashion as a reflection of society and politics continued into the 90's, bringing about new trends.

2000's

“Fashion is in the sky, in the street, fashion has to do with ideas, the way we live, what is happening.”

The 2000's was represented by bold, saturated colors and exploration of eccentric trends that quickly came in and out of style.

2010's

“Fashion is part of our culture, and it's about more than just a pretty dress.”

The 2010's have thus far been defined by athleisure, a revival of past period pieces and alternative fashions.
